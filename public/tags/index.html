Thinking Statistically: When Data Visualizations Lie to You

If there’s one thing I’ve learned by going into statistics as a career, it’s that it’s so easy to “lie” with data. Visualizations are one of the easiest ways to get away with doing this and tell a story that in all actuality may not be true at all. Edward Tufte was a statistician and artist, and he was acutely aware of this problem too. He’s known for writing books such as The Visual Display of Quantitative Information, which is a classic regarding how to create statistical visualization.
